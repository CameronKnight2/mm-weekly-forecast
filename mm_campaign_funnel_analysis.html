Campaign attribution gap

1. MQL Volume Cliff in February — Investigate Immediately

  • MQLs dropped 52% month-over-month from 28,283 (Jan) to 13,564 (Feb). This is the single biggest risk to Q3 pipeline generation.
  • Root cause: Multiple high-volume Q2 campaigns ended (KAM Blitz, Payroll Switcher variants) and were not replaced with equivalent Q3 campaigns. The KAM Blitz alone contributed 8,493 MQLs in Dec and then went to zero.
  • Action: Demand gen leadership should launch Q3 replacement campaigns immediately. The IES Hot List Relevvo and NBA plays are ramping but at 500–1,500 MQL scale — not enough to fill the 15K gap.

2. IES IPD Abandoner Campaigns — High Volume, Low Pipeline Conversion

  • The three IPD Abandoner BDR campaigns generated 19,505 MQLs (27% of total) but only produced $6.1M in pipeline across 540 opps with a Sol+ rate of just 10%.
  • Compare to Web Managed IES: 722 opps but $118M pipeline with a 19% Sol+ rate — 20x the pipeline-per-opp efficiency.
  • Action: Re-evaluate the IPD Abandoner audience quality. Consider tighter scoring criteria or shifting BDR capacity to higher-converting campaigns like Web Managed IES or Accountant Pod Co-Sell.

3. Accountant Channel is a Pipeline Powerhouse — Under-Invested

  • Accountant-driven campaigns (Pod Co-Sell + Lead Pass + Client Driven + Desk Reg) generated $54M in pipeline with a 30% Sol+ rate on Co-Sell and 25% on Client Driven.
  • Yet Accountant MQLs are tiny: only 203 → 44 → 1,040 across the 3 months. The Feb spike to 1,040 suggests new Accountant campaigns are just starting.
  • Action: Double down on Accountant channel. The Sol+ conversion rates are 2–3x better than BDR outbound campaigns. Expand the Virtual Connect and IES Pod Co-Sell programs.

4. Pipeline Aging Is Increasing — Deals Stalling in Assess/Solution

  • Average open opportunity age increased from 76.8 days (Dec) to 81.5 days (Jan) and stayed at 80.7 in Feb. Opps are sitting in early stages longer.
  • The "No Campaign Identified" bucket has 431 opps with $11.3M pipeline — these are attribution gaps that prevent campaign ROI analysis.
  • Action: Implement pipeline velocity reviews focused on opps >60 days in Assess/Solution. Fix campaign attribution for the 1,311 "No Campaign" opps to close the measurement gap.

5. IES Renewal Opportunity — Highest Sol+ Rate, Protect This Pipeline

  • IES Renewal has the highest Sol+ rate at 47.6% with 88 of 185 opps in Sol+ stages and $3.8M in Commit.
  • This is the most efficient pipeline source — renewals convert at nearly 5x the rate of BDR-sourced campaigns.
  • Action: Ensure renewal opps have dedicated AM coverage. Any slippage here directly impacts IES NRR. Consider a renewal-specific blitz in Q3.

6. Trade Show ROI — IFA Feb 2026 Showing Outsized Returns

  • The IFA Trade Show Hand Raiser campaign has only 7 opps but $5.8M in pipeline — average opp size of $829K, which is 340x the overall average of $2,323.
  • IBS Trade Show also performing well with 51 opps and $1.6M pipeline at 43% Sol+ rate.
  • Action: Prioritize trade show follow-up. These are high-value, high-intent leads. Ensure dedicated AMs are assigned within 48 hours of event.
